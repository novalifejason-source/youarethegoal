What is this & why it matters

The Wheel of Life is one of the most powerful self-assessment tools used by coaches and leaders worldwide. It gives you a clear visual snapshot of how balanced — or unbalanced — your life truly is right now. Think of your life as a wheel: when all areas are strong and roughly equal, the wheel rolls smoothly forward. When some areas are high and others are neglected, the wheel wobbles — and that wobble shows up as stress, frustration, or the nagging feeling that something is missing, even when you're working hard.

The scoring scale
1Very far from your ideal. This area feels neglected, painful, or seriously lacking.
10Fully living your ideal. Thriving, not just surviving.
Before you score

A 10 is not perfection — it means you are genuinely living your ideal version of this area right now. Not compared to others. What truly feels right and fulfilling for you. Be honest. Score where you actually are today, not where you hope to be. Real insights come from honesty, not optimism.

Step 1 — Before scoring, define what a "10" looks like for you in each area
Career / Professional
Purpose, growth & satisfaction in your work. Leading with confidence, excited to start each day.
Finances / Money
Financial security, freedom & abundance. Savings, debt-free, investing with confidence.
Health / Well-Being
Physical energy, fitness, nutrition & sleep. Waking up strong and rested every morning.
Relationships / Family
Deep, nourishing connections. Feeling truly seen, supported & close to the people who matter.
Personal Growth
Continuous learning, skill-building & mindset development. Growing in self-awareness daily.
Fun / Leisure
Joy, play & hobbies that light you up. Guilt-free time to rest and do what you love.
Spirituality / Faith
Inner peace, connection to your values & a sense of deeper meaning that guides your decisions.
Environment / Home
A calm, organized space that energizes and supports your focus rather than drains you.

Your Reflection

The one question that changes everything.

Step 4 — The one question

"If nothing changes in the next 5 years —
how does that feel?"

Close your eyes. Sit with it for 30 seconds. Then write your raw, honest reaction below.
Step 5 — Read your wheel
Which area scored lowest — and is that a surprise to you?
Which one area, if raised by just 2–3 points in the next 90 days, would create the biggest ripple effect across your whole life?
Where are you already stronger than you give yourself credit for? What does that tell you about what you're capable of?
If your entire wheel is low and roughly even — does it feel "balanced" or "stuck"? What story are you telling yourself about why life is just okay?
The gap shows where you're going. The gain shows who you already are. This is where your next 90 days begins.
